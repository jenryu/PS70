week 3

jack in the box!

this week, I really wanted to make something that incorporated some sort of linear actuator. i made a homemade linear actuator with a servo motor, some straws, and a thread screw. as i was brainstorming what to create to move something up and down, i got inspired by the idea of a whack a mole, but decided to take it a step further by creating a jack in the box.

i already had the linear actuator made with the servo motor, so making the “jack” move up and down was pretty straightforward. all i needed was to make a box with some cute decorations. however, i decided that i wanted a handheld crank, like most jack in the boxes. i needed to figure out how to connect the bottom rotation of the servo into a horizontal rotation on the side of the box.

this was quite challenging since i didn’t know how to 3d print yet, but after doing some research, it looked like bevel gears are the usual method to change the angle of rotation. after trying various things, i randomly found bevel gears thrown away in some random box in the lab late at night. it didn’t fit quite right, but i tried my best to make it fit.

then i made a little clay capybara and bam! jack in the box!
